I have a Bachelor's Degree from the Industrial Design program at Emily Carr University of Art and Design and I'm a design manager working for Innovative Signage.

In these past few years, I have worked closely with designers and account managers. In doing this, I have found that I'm fascinated by the UX process and how it can be a critical turning point of a product, or service. I love human behavior and problem solving, and I get excited about how technology can improve people’s lives.

I spend my spare time learning about different UX processes, concepts and theories through attending workshops, reading blogs and books and applying these learnings in my everyday life, in my projects I work on in my spare time and in my daily work.

WHAT I OFFER

User Experience design is the commitment to designing procucts that are useful, easy to use, enjoyable to interact with and are created with the customer in mind. It's about enhancing the experience that people have while using your procuct and making sure they finc value in what you provice.

A brand identity is made up of what your brand says, what your values are, how you communicate your product and what you want people to feel when they interact with it. Essentially, your brand identity is the personality of your business and a promise to your customers. A brand is what makes you memorable.

Visual designshapes and improves the user experience by focusing on color, images, fonts and other visual elements. A successful visual design ensures that content remains central to the product’s function and enhances it by engaging users and helping to build their trust and interest in the product.

LANDMARK ON ROBSON

When in a parkade, it can be very confusing to know how to get to your destination if there are not clear markers that tell a person where to go. This means a person could either get lost or arrive at a different destination than was intended. In addition to the parkade, we added the scope of a signage guideline for the retail sector of the buildings. This allows for the building to set a set of rules for the signage used for the retail units within the building.
